BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN ELECTRONICS ENGINEERING (BSECE)
 
 

PROGRAM OBJECTIVES

•To provide the students a solid foundation in science and electronics engineering.
• To provide the students an in-depth understanding of the electronics engineering process.
• To develop student's ability to integrate new computing tools in electronics engineering process.
• To place emphasis on student's concern for the environment.
• To create awareness among the faculty and students on ethical issues in electronics engineering.
• To develop the student's ability to relate electronics engineering to other disciplines.

PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

The Electronics Engineering program provides an infrastructure that will allow the full development of the student in preparation for professional life as an electronics and communications engineer. Its curriculum provides diverse activities and opportunities in the fields of telecommunications and electronics engineering. This program includes such topics as device physics, device operation, design of integrated circuits, communications and network systems, audio and video processing, robotics, electromagnetics and antenna systems, and electronic materials to name a few.
 

STUDENT ORGANIZATION

ORGANIZATION OF ELECTRONICS ENGINEERING STUDENTS (OECES)

The Organization of Electronics Engineering Students (OECES) at Technological University of the Philippines (TUP) Manila is dedicated to nurturing excellence and fostering a sense of community among aspiring electronics engineers. Through a diverse array of initiatives including seminars, workshops, competitions, and social events, OECES enhances theoretical learning with practical experiences and promotes mentorship among its members. Beyond the university, OECES extends its impact through outreach programs and partnerships, advocating for the pivotal role of electronics engineering in driving innovation and addressing societal challenges. In essence, OECES empowers its members to excel professionally and make meaningful contributions to society.
